Working Principle of DC Motors

How DC Motors Work

DC motors convert electrical energy into mechanical energy using the interaction of magnetic fields. When current flows through the armature coil, it creates a magnetic field that interacts with the stator field, producing torque that rotates the motor shaft.

Main Components

  • Stator: Provides the stationary magnetic field.
  • Rotor (Armature): Rotates to produce mechanical motion.
  • Commutator: Reverses current direction to maintain rotation.
  • Brushes: Conduct current between the stationary and rotating parts.

Step-by-Step Operation

When DC voltage is applied, current flows through the armature. The resulting magnetic field interacts with the stator field, creating a force. This force generates torque, causing rotation. The commutator ensures continuous rotation by switching the current direction at appropriate intervals.
